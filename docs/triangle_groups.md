# Triangle Groups and the (2,p,q) Triangle Groups

## What is a Triangle Group?

A triangle group `(l, m, n)` is a group generated by three reflections `r₁, r₂, r₃`
across the sides of a triangle with angles `π/l, π/m, π/n`.

```
Relations: r₁² = r₂² = r₃² = 1
           (r₁r₂)ˡ = 1
           (r₂r₃)ᵐ = 1
           (r₁r₃)ⁿ = 1
```

The sum `1/l + 1/m + 1/n` determines the geometry:
| Sum | Geometry | Examples |
|-----|----------|---------|
| > 1 | Spherical | (2,2,n), (2,3,3), (2,3,4), (2,3,5) |
| = 1 | Euclidean | (2,3,6), (2,4,4), (3,3,3) |
| < 1 | Hyperbolic | (2,3,7), (2,4,5), (2,3,8), … |

## The (2,p,q) Triangle Groups

For regular tilings {p,q}, the relevant group is `(2,p,q)`:
- Angle π/2 at the right-angle vertex (edge midpoint)
- Angle π/p at the p-vertex (polygon center)
- Angle π/q at the q-vertex (tiling vertex)

### Generators as Reflections

The three generators correspond to:
1. `r₁`: Reflection across the "hypotenuse" geodesic (the **tile boundary** — these are {p,q} edges)
2. `r₂`: Reflection across the leg adjacent to the p-vertex (interior symmetry of a tile)
3. `r₃`: Reflection across the leg adjacent to the q-vertex (interior symmetry)

### Rotations

The products of consecutive reflections give rotations:
- `r₂r₁` = rotation of order p about the p-vertex (p-fold symmetry of polygon)
- `r₃r₁` = rotation of order q about the q-vertex (q-fold symmetry at vertex)
- `r₃r₂` = rotation of order 2 about the right-angle vertex (2-fold = half-turn)

## Key Triangle Groups for This Project

### (2,3,7) — Hurwitz Group Family

The most famous. Tightest hyperbolic triangle (area π/42, the minimum for a compact quotient).

```
1/2 + 1/3 + 1/7 = 21/42 + 14/42 + 6/42 = 41/42  < 1
Defect = 1 - 41/42 = 1/42
Area = π/42
```

**Significance**: Quotient by torsion-free subgroup of index 168 → Klein quartic.
This gives the **maximum** number of symmetries for a genus-3 surface: 168 = 84(g-1).
This is the hyperbolic analogue of Hurwitz's theorem: any curve of genus g ≥ 2
has at most 84(g-1) automorphisms.

**The (2,3,7) group tiles**: {7,3} and {3,7}

### (2,4,5) — Escher Group

```
1/2 + 1/4 + 1/5 = 10/20 + 5/20 + 4/20 = 19/20
Defect = 1/20
Area = π/20  [Wait, area = π·defect for fundamental domain... let me recalculate]
```

Wait, for a triangle with angles α,β,γ, area = π - α - β - γ:
Area(2,4,5) = π - π/2 - π/4 - π/5 = π(1 - 1/2 - 1/4 - 1/5) = π(20-10-5-4)/20 = π/20

**The (2,4,5) group tiles**: {5,4} and {4,5}  
**Famous for**: Escher's Circle Limit IV (Angels and Demons)

### (2,4,6) — Clean Hexagonal Group

Area(2,4,6) = π(1 - 1/2 - 1/4 - 1/6) = π(12-6-3-2)/12 = π/12

Has exact arithmetic (D = 1/4 in the fundamental triangle).

**The (2,4,6) group tiles**: {6,4} and {4,6}

### (2,3,∞) — Ideal Triangle Group

One vertex is ideal (on the boundary). The "triangle" has an infinite area cusp.
Area = π(1 - 1/2 - 1/3) = π/6  (infinite domain, but sector has finite area)

**The (2,3,∞) group tiles**: {∞,3} (apeirogonal tiling)

## Building the Full Tiling from the Triangle Group

### Step 1: Choose Fundamental Triangle
For {p,q}: use the right triangle with angles π/2, π/p, π/q.

### Step 2: Apply All Reflections
Each image of the fundamental triangle under the group is one of the 2pq triangles
per fundamental domain of the orientation-preserving subgroup.

### Step 3: Group Triangles into Tiles
The p-gon (tile) in {p,q} consists of 2q fundamental triangles, all sharing the p-vertex.
The full tiling has q such tiles meeting at each q-vertex.

### Step 4: Color by Parity
The parity of the number of arc reflections gives the two-coloring of the {p,q} tiling.

## The Rotation Subgroup

The **orientation-preserving** subgroup (index 2 subgroup) of a triangle group is generated
by the rotation elements:
- `S = r₂r₁` (order p)
- `T = r₃r₁` (order q)

This is the **conformal symmetry group** of the tiling, excluding reflections.

For (2,3,7): the rotation subgroup has presentation `⟨S, T | S⁷ = T³ = (ST)² = 1⟩`
This is the von Dyck group D(2,3,7).

## Cayley Graph

The Cayley graph of a triangle group G with generators {r₁, r₂, r₃}:
- **Vertices**: group elements (= fundamental triangle images)
- **Edges**: apply a generator (move to adjacent triangle)
- **Structure**: infinite planar graph embedded in the hyperbolic disk

The Cayley graph of the (2,3,7) rotation subgroup:
- Vertices labeled by tiles (one per heptagon)
- Each vertex connects to 7 neighbors
- This IS the {7,3} combinatorial structure
